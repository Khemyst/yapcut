# YouTube Editing Knowledge Base

Claude reads this before every editing session. These are data-backed editing principles compiled from MrBeast's production framework, YouTube retention research, and professional editing best practices.

## The MrBeast Pacing Shift (2024-2025)

MrBeast reversed course on "retention editing" (rapid cuts, loud sounds, visual chaos):
- **2023**: 38 cuts per 60 seconds
- **2024**: 23 cuts per 60 seconds (39% reduction)
- Result: views increased from ~60M to ~150M average over 90 days
- His guidance: abandon the "ultra fast paced/overstim era" because "it doesn't even work"
- The shift: less screaming, slower pacing, more story, more emotion, more personality
- 45% of YouTube viewership now happens on TVs where rapid-fire editing is unnecessary

**Takeaway**: Optimal pacing is NOT maximum cuts. It is intentional rhythm -- fast when energy demands it, slow when story demands it.

## Minute-by-Minute Video Structure

| Timeframe | Objective |
|---|---|
| **0:00 - 1:00** | Hook. Maximum impact. Front-load stimulation. Match the thumbnail promise. 55%+ of viewers leave in the first 60 seconds -- this is normal. |
| **1:00 - 3:00** | Crazy progression. Show, don't tell. Cover ground fast. Get viewers emotionally invested. |
| **3:00** | First re-engagement spectacle. Viewers start getting bored. Reset attention with something unexpected. |
| **3:00 - 6:00** | Story investment with rapid scene changes. Make the audience care about the people and the journey. |
| **6:00** | Second re-engagement. Deeper explanation is now permitted. Push the story into the back half. |
| **6:00 - End** | Invested viewers. Longer explanations survive here. Maintain equal quality but pacing can breathe more. |
| **Ending** | End abruptly. NEVER signal the video is ending. Signaling causes immediate retention drops. |

### Key Drop-Off Points
- **0:08** -- First major stay-or-leave decision
- **0:30** -- Hook window closes; most who will leave do so here
- **1:00** -- 55%+ already gone
- **3:00** -- Boredom onset for survivors

## Seven Retention Strategies

1. **Escalating stakes** -- progressive difficulty/value throughout
2. **Format rotation** -- never repeat the same energy consecutively
3. **Storytelling mastery** -- tension building and narrative structure
4. **Unique spectacle** -- moments competitors cannot replicate
5. **Open loops** -- unresolved questions that sustain curiosity (+32% watch time)
6. **Pattern interrupts** -- unexpected changes that reset attention (+23% retention)
7. **Re-hooks every 2-3 minutes** -- tease upcoming content, deliver a quick win, or shift energy

## Hook Structure

### Long-Form (First 30 Seconds)
| Phase | Timing | Objective |
|---|---|---|
| Attention Grab | 0:00 - 0:05 | Pattern interrupt, bold visual, provocative statement |
| Clarify Promise | 0:05 - 0:15 | State what the viewer will get |
| Establish Stakes | 0:15 - 0:30 | Begin the journey, raise emotional stakes |

### Short-Form (First 3 Seconds)
- 50-60% of Short drop-offs happen in the first 3 seconds
- Below 50% hook retention = failed hook
- Open with peak energy, mid-sentence, or a reaction in progress
- No setup runway

### What Kills a Hook
- "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel"
- Talking about what you WILL do instead of doing it
- Logos, animated intros, brand bumpers before the hook
- Any delay in delivering value

## Energy Management (Peaks and Valleys)

Monotonous pacing at ANY speed disengages the audience. The contrast between peaks and valleys creates engagement, not constant speed.

**The Rollercoaster Model:**
- Default neutral pace (15-25 second shots for talking head)
- Every 2-3 minutes: burst sequence of 5-10 quick cuts (reactions, scene shifts)
- Return to neutral after each burst
- Something visually meaningful every 3-5 seconds (angle change, B-roll, graphic)

**Sound Design Integration:**
- Calm/narrative: background music at -20 to -25 dB below voice
- Energetic sequences: -8 to -12 dB
- Teaching moments: 60-80 BPM
- Build-ups/transitions: 100-120 BPM
- Strategic silence before major reveals amplifies impact

## Cut Types and When to Use Them

**Jump Cut**: Remove fillers, compress time, comedy surprise. The default YouTube cut.

**J-Cut** (audio leads video): Create intrigue before revealing the visual. Effective for intros and segment transitions. Creates urgency.

**L-Cut** (video cuts, audio continues): Let a scene linger emotionally. Show reaction shots while dialogue continues. Good for cliffhanger transitions.

## Gaming/Streaming Specifics

### VOD-to-YouTube Compression
- 4-hour stream → 15-25 minutes of highlight content (10:1 to 16:1)
- Reset viewer attention every 1-2 minutes with pacing shifts
- Gaming content tolerates faster cutting (high visual information density)
- Audio: never let game audio overpower voice

### Implicit Cuts (Never Mark)
- Loading screens, matchmaking, menu navigation
- Dead air with no vocal or gameplay activity
- Repetitive gameplay loops without commentary variation
- Donation/subscriber reads (unless chat reaction is exceptional)
- AFK moments, bathroom breaks, tech difficulties

### What to Keep
- Vocal energy peaks (sudden yelling, laughter, disbelief)
- Chat density spikes (sudden message bursts = something happened)
- Mechanical peaks (clutch plays, unlikely kills, funny fails)
- Strong interpersonal moments (banter, arguments, callouts)
- Genuine emotional reactions
- Silence → vocal explosion (the silence IS content; extend cut backwards)

### Caption Timing
- Appear 0.1-0.3 seconds before corresponding audio
- Each block: one complete thought, 2-4 seconds
- Position: center third of screen (avoid top 20%, bottom 25%)
- Font: sans-serif, 18-24pt, solid black box at 60-80% opacity

## The Hierarchy of Editing Decisions

When deciding whether to keep or cut a segment:

1. **Does it serve the story?** If no, cut it.
2. **Does it maintain or increase energy?** If it creates a flat zone without recovery, cut or compress.
3. **Would the viewer understand the next segment without it?** If yes, expendable. If no, it is context.
4. **Does it duplicate another moment?** Keep the better version, cut the weaker one.
5. **Does audience reaction validate it?** Use chat/engagement as tiebreaker for ambiguous moments.

## The Non-Negotiable Rules

1. First 8 seconds determine whether viewers stay. Open with maximum impact.
2. State value proposition within first 15 seconds.
3. Never signal the video is ending. End abruptly.
4. Re-hook every 2-3 minutes.
5. Vary pacing. Monotony at any speed loses viewers.
6. Cut on emotional beats, not arbitrary intervals.
7. Open loops increase watch time by 32%.
8. Pattern interrupts boost retention by 23%.
9. Sound design is not optional.
10. For short-form: hook in under 3 seconds or lose 50-60% of your audience.
